Georgia O’Keeffe, Artist

Gelatin silver print by Alfred Stieglitz (1929) © Georgia O’Keeffe Museum

Born in Sun Prairie, Wisconsin, in 1887, O’Keeffe developed an interest in art while she was still a child. By the time she died in 1986, she had become one of the most famous painters of the 20th century. Her works hang in museums around the world.

Timeline of the artist’s life

1887
Born in Wisconsin; her parents were dairy farmers. She had six siblings.
1901 – 1902
Attended boarding school in Madison, Wisconsin.
1903
Joined her family in Williamsburg, Virginia, where they had moved in 1902.
1905 – 1906
Attended the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.
1907
Attended the Art Students League in New York City.
1908
Met her future husband, photographer Alfred Stieglitz, at his gallery “291,” during an exhibition of Rodin's watercolors.
1912
Took up painting again after a four-year hiatus while working in Chicago as a commercial artist.
1912 – 1914
Taught art in the public schools in Amarillo, Texas.
1914 – 1916
Attended Teachers College of Columbia University, where she took classes from Arthur Wesley Dow.
1916 – 1918
Took a job as head of the art department at West Texas State Normal College, just south of Amarillo.
1918
Moved to New York to devote all of her time to painting and began living together with Stieglitz.
1929
Traveled to Santa Fe, New Mexico, with her friend Rebecca Strand. Moved into Mabel Dodge Luhan's house in Taos.
1932
Suffered a nervous breakdown.
1940
Moved to Ghost Ranch, north of Abiquiú, New Mexico.
1986
Died in Santa Fe, New Mexico.
